# Phishing Email Investigation Report

## Platform / Source
Public phishing email example used for security analysis and phishing awareness practice.

## Objective
Analyze a phishing email example to identify social engineering tactics, credential theft indicators, and suspicious warning signs commonly used in phishing attacks.

## Skills Practiced
- Phishing email analysis
- Threat identification
- Social engineering awareness
- Security documentation

## Threat Identified
The phishing email used urgency and account-security language to pressure the user into taking action. It attempted to create trust through PayPal branding while directing the user toward a “Resolution Center” style login flow.

## Red Flags Observed
- Urgent language such as **“Response required”**
- Account scare tactic involving **unusual login activity**
- Request to log in and resolve the issue through a provided account link
- Potential risk of credential harvesting through a fake PayPal login page

## Impact
A successful phishing attempt could lead to credential theft, account compromise, fraud, and unauthorized access to financial or personal information.

## Recommended Actions
- Do not click suspicious links or attachments
- Verify the sender independently through official channels
- Report the email to security teams
- Use MFA and reset credentials if the user interacted with the message

## Evidence
Screenshots included showing the phishing email, the phishing overview page, and suspicious indicators identified during analysis.
